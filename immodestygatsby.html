A Spectacle of Excess: Immodesty in Baz Luhrmann’s The Great Gatsby

Baz Luhrmann’s adaptation heightens the novel’s focus on excess, glamour, and materialism, aligning with an immodest style. Lavish parties, flamboyant costumes, and modern music underscore a culture obsessed with status. Gatsby’s obsessive dream and Daisy and Tom’s moral emptiness reveal how showy indulgence conceals a hollow pursuit of the American Dream—ultimately exposing the tragic allure of immodesty in 1920s high society.
