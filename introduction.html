Evolution of Clothing

Clothing began with animal skins for warmth and protection. Simple plant fibers followed, with bone needles enabling stitching and more precise garments.

Ancient Egyptians favored linen, Mesopotamians used wool, and the Indus Valley popularized cotton. The Middle Ages saw class distinctions shape fashion through tailoring and expensive dyes.

The Industrial Revolution mechanized textile production, making clothing cheaper and more widespread. Modern fashion prioritizes innovation in fabrics and style, with growing emphasis on sustainability and ethical production.
